---
title: "Asynchronous RSI Practices"
---

Here is a list of asynchronous activities that qualify as Regular Substantive Interaction (RSI). They ensure proactive and consistent interactions amongst all class members.

These activities are built on the Community of Inquiry framework, which posits that "Teaching Presence" — the instructor's active facilitation of the course — is the essential glue that binds social engagement and cognitive development together in an online space.

## Facilitated Discussion Forums 
Rather than a "post once, reply twice" requirement, the instructor actively monitors threads, asks probing follow-up questions to specific students, and challenges assumptions to deepen the academic rigor.

## Weekly Synthesis Announcements 
At the end of a module, the instructor posts a video or detailed summary that synthesizes student contributions, highlights specific high-quality insights shared in class, and clarifies common misconceptions.

## Personalized Video or Audio Feedback
Instead of just a rubric score, the instructor provides recorded feedback on major assignments, offering tailored academic guidance and suggestions for future improvement.

## Moderated Small-Group Check-ins 
If students are working on long-term projects, the instructor provides "substantive" guidance by visiting group workspaces or shared documents to offer feedback on their progress and direction.

## "Muddiest Point" Explainer Videos
Based on quiz results or forum questions, the instructor creates a supplemental video specifically designed to clarify the most difficult or misunderstood concept of the week.  

## Collaborative Document Annotation 
Using tools like Google Docs, Perusall or Hypothesis, the instructor actively participates in the margins of a text, adding academic context and responding to student annotations in real-time.
